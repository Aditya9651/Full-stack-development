TYPES OF FORCES

FRICTION

Friction is the force that opposes the motion of objects sliding or moving past each other. It occurs when two surfaces come into contact, and the microscopic bumps and irregularities on each surface catch and resist movement. Friction is essential in everyday life because it allows us to walk without slipping and cars to grip the road. However, it can also cause wear and tear on mechanical parts and reduce efficiency by turning some energy into heat. Understanding friction helps engineers design better machines and materials that either reduce or increase this force, depending on the need.

STRESS

Stress is the bodys natural response to challenges or demands, whether they come from work, relationships, or unexpected situations. When you experience stress, your brain signals your body to release hormones like adrenaline and cortisol, preparing you to react quickly—a reaction often called “fight or flight.”
While a little stress can motivate you to perform better, chronic or excessive stress can harm your health, leading to problems like anxiety, headaches, and fatigue. Managing stress through relaxation techniques, exercise, and time management is important for maintaining both mental and physical well-being.

STRAIN

Physical/Muscle strain:

An injury to a muscle or tendon caused by overstretching or excessive force, often resulting in pain and limited movement.

Mechanical strain:

The deformation or displacement of material that results from applied stress (force). Its usually expressed as a ratio or percentage of change in length compared to the original length.

Biological strain:

A genetic variant or subtype of a microorganism, such as a virus, bacterium, or plant.

Emotional/mental strain:

Stress or pressure experienced mentally or emotionally
